# IBKR Canada Picks-and-Shovels Investor MVP Plan

## Summary
- Build a greenfield, single-user app for personal use: `IBKR Canada` first, with programmatic trading limited to `US-listed stocks and ETFs`.
- Strategy: invest in differentiated suppliers to fast-growing spend waves, not the eventual downstream winner and not on technical-analysis signals.
- V1 theme focus: AI infrastructure picks-and-shovels such as compute, networking, power/cooling, foundry/packaging, and related infrastructure bottlenecks.
- Runtime: your machine only. Interface is a local web dashboard with desktop notifications.
- Rollout: IBKR paper trading first, then `live_capped`; live mode auto-executes only small trades inside hard limits.
- Holding period: days to weeks, tied to filings, earnings, capex, demand, backlog, and guidance trends rather than intraday price movement.

## Implementation Changes
- Broker and market access:
  - Use `Interactive Brokers Canada` as the live broker and primary brokerage integration.
  - Use `IBKR Web API` or `TWS API` for automation, account state, orders, positions, and execution events.
  - Constrain the investable universe to `US-listed securities only`.
  - Add a hard `reject_canadian_listing` rule because IBKR's API docs say Canadian residents are restricted from programmatically trading products traded on Canadian exchanges/marketplaces.
  - Treat `TSX`, `TSXV`, `CSE`, `NEO`, and other Canadian-listed instruments as out of scope for v1 automation.
- Strategy engine:
  - Explicitly exclude `RSI`, `MACD`, moving-average crossovers, beta screens, candlestick patterns, and other technical-analysis signals from v1.
  - Define a `ThemeDefinition` for each investable wave with fields such as `theme_name`, `spend_driver`, `enablement_layer`, `winner_agnostic_case`, and `disqualifiers`.
  - Score each company on six axes: `theme_linkage`, `multi_winner_exposure`, `bottleneck_or_differentiation`, `growth_proof`, `management_proof`, and `valuation_sanity`.
  - Add hard `reject_ubiquity`, `reject_commodity`, and `reject_non_us_listing` rules before any live order can be generated.
- Research and evidence pipeline:
  - Ingest official investor-relations releases, earnings slides, annual reports, 10-K/10-Q/20-F filings, and market news for each approved company.
  - Normalize evidence into structured records with `source_type`, `publish_date`, `metric`, `quoted_growth`, `theme_relevance`, and `confidence`.
  - Maintain a manually approved seed universe of 10 to 20 `US-listed` names rather than scanning the whole market.
  - Re-rank companies after earnings, guidance changes, major capex announcements, or material backlog/order updates.
- Portfolio and execution:
  - V1 is `long-only`, `whole-share only`, `stocks/ETFs only`, `regular-hours only`, `no options`, `no shorting`, `no margin`, and `no extended-hours orders`.
  - Portfolio construction favors a small basket of enablers rather than a single-name bet on the downstream application winner.
  - Rebalance cadence is event-driven and daily, not minute-by-minute.
  - Risk engine is the final gate and can only approve orders if bankroll, concentration, liquidity, spread, cooldown, and daily-loss checks pass.
  - Execution service supports `paused`, `paper`, and `live_capped`, reconciles fills on restart, and only places orders generated by the theme strategy.
- Frontend and persistence:
  - Dashboard pages cover themes, approved universe, evidence, company scores, positions, decisions, broker health, alerts, and run-mode controls.
  - `SQLite` stores themes, companies, evidence records, scores, order intents, fills, positions, alerts, and audit logs.
  - IBKR credentials/session details and model credentials live in the OS keychain; the database stores only masked references.

## Public Interfaces / Types
- Core interfaces:
  - `BrokerAdapter`: `get_account()`, `get_clock()`, `list_positions()`, `submit_order()`, `cancel_order()`, `list_open_orders()`, `stream_order_updates()`
  - `ResearchSource`: `fetch_items(company)`, `normalize_item()`, `extract_evidence()`
  - `ThemeEngine`: `score_company(symbol, theme_id) -> ThemeCompanyScore`
  - `DecisionEngine`: `build_position_actions(theme_id) -> list[Decision]`
  - `RiskEngine`: `evaluate(intent, portfolio_state) -> Allow | Block(reason[])`
- Strategy types:
  - `ThemeDefinition`: theme thesis, spend driver, enablement layer, winner-agnostic case, disqualifiers
  - `EvidenceRecord`: source metadata plus extracted growth/order/backlog/guidance evidence
  - `ThemeCompanyScore`: normalized scorecard with component scores and rejection reasons
  - `Decision`: symbol, side, target_weight, max_notional, thesis, invalidation_rules, review_date
- Backend API:
  - `GET /health`, `GET /themes`, `GET /companies`, `POST /watchlist`, `GET /positions`, `GET /decisions`, `GET /alerts`
  - `POST /mode` with `paused|paper|live_capped`, `POST /risk-limits`, `POST /themes`, `POST /broker/test`
  - `WS /events` for evidence updates, score changes, decisions, fills, broker status, and alerts
- Default v1 limits:
  - bankroll cap `$250`
  - max order size `min($25, 10% of bankroll)`
  - max `5` open names
  - max `2` names per theme
  - max daily loss `$25`
  - block trades in illiquid names, non-US-listed names, names below the configured minimum price, or names that fail the thesis-quality checks

## Test Plan
- Unit tests to verify technical indicators and beta inputs are never read by the decision engine.
- Unit tests for each score axis and each hard-reject rule, especially `reject_ubiquity`, `reject_commodity`, and `reject_non_us_listing`.
- Positive-case tests for approved picks-and-shovels names when evidence shows accelerating demand, backlog, or theme-linked growth.
- Negative-case tests for Canadian-listed symbols and broad commodity or generic-necessity businesses.
- Replay tests using captured filings, earnings releases, news items, and IBKR order events to verify evidence extraction and broker-state reconciliation.
- Paper-mode integration test: new evidence -> score update -> decision -> risk pass/block -> simulated IBKR fill -> dashboard update.
- Acceptance gates before live:
  - at least `2 weeks` of paper trading on the approved universe
  - zero reconciliation mismatches after restart
  - zero malformed model outputs escaping schema validation
  - manual review of the first live config with notifications enabled

## Assumptions / Defaults
- Personal-use app, not multi-user SaaS.
- Seed theme for v1 is `AI picks-and-shovels`, not a broad market allocator.
- The investable universe is restricted to `US-listed stocks and ETFs` because the user is in Canada and IBKR programmatic trading of Canadian-listed products is out of scope.
- Starter company examples that fit the thesis and are US-listed include `NVDA`, `ANET`, `VRT`, `AVGO`, and `TSM`.
- A company can still fail the strategy if the valuation check, concentration rules, or evidence quality are weak.
